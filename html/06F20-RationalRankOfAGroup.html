rational rank of a group


In the following, G is an abelian group.

Definition 1.

The group Q⊗ZG is called the divisible hull of G.

It is a ℚ-vector space such that the scalar ℤ-multiplication of G is extended to ℚ.

Definition 2.

The elements g1,g2,…,gr∈G are called rationally independent if they are linearly independent over Z, i.e. for all n1,…,nr∈Z:

n1⁢g1+…+nr⁢gr=0⇒n1=…=nr=0.
Definition 3.

The dimension of Q⊗ZG over Q is called the rational rank of G.

We denote the rational rank of G by r⁢(G).
Example:
r⁢(ℤ×ℤ)=2 because ℚ⊗ℤ(ℤ×ℤ)=(ℚ⊗ℤℤ)×(ℚ⊗ℤℤ)=ℚ×ℚ.
Properties:

  • •

    If H is a subgroup of G then we have:

    r⁢(G)=r⁢(H)+r⁢(G/H).

    It results from the fact that ℚℤ is a flat module.

  • •

    The rational rank of the group G can be defined as the least upper bound (finite or infinite) of the cardinals r such that there exist r rationally independent elements in G.
