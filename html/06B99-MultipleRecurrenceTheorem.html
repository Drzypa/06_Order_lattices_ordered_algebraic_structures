Multiple Recurrence Theorem


Let (X,ℬ,μ) be a probability space, and let Ti:X→X be measure-preserving transformations, for i between 1 and q. Assume that all the transformations Ti commute. If E⊂X is a positive measure set μ⁢(E)>0, then, there exists n∈ℕ such that

μ⁢(E∩T1-n⁢(E)∩⋯∩Tq-n⁢(E))>0

In other words there exist a certain time n such that the subset of E for which all elements return to E simultaneously for all transformations Ti is a subset of E with positive measure. Observe that the theorem may be applied again to the set G=E∩T1-n⁢(E)∩⋯∩Tq-n⁢(E), obtaining the existence of m∈ℕ such that

μ⁢(G∩T1-m⁢(G)∩⋯∩Tq-m⁢(G))>0

so that

μ⁢(E∩T1-(m+n)⁢(E)∩⋯∩Tq-(m+n)⁢(E))≥μ⁢(G∩T1-m⁢(G)∩⋯∩Tq-m⁢(G))>0

So we may conclude that, when E has positive measure, there are infinite times for which there is a simultaneous return for a subset of E with positive measure.

As a corollary, since the powers T,T2⁢⋯⁢Tq of a transformation T commute, we have that, for E with positive measure there exists n∈ℕ such that

μ⁢(E∩T-n⁢(E)∩⋯∩T-q⁢n⁢(E))>0
