semimodular lattice


A lattice L is semimodular 11Or upper semimodular, if one wants to stress the distinction with lower semimodular lattices. if for any a and b∈L,

a∧b≺a implies b≺a∨b,

where ≺ denotes the covering relation in L. Dually, a lattice L is said to be lower semimodular if for any a and b∈L,

b≺a∨b implies a∧b≺a.

A chain finite lattice is modular (http://planetmath.org/ModularLattice) if and only if it is both semimodular and lower semimodular.

The smallest lattice which is semimodular but not modular is

since d≤a but a∧(c∨d)≠(a∧c)∨d.
