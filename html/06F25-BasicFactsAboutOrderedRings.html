basic facts about ordered rings


Throughout this entry, (R,≤) is an ordered ring.

Lemma 1.

If a,b,c∈R with a<b, then a+c<b+c.

Proof.

The contrapositive will be proven.

Let a,b,c∈R with a+c≥b+c. Note that -c∈R. Thus,

b=b+0=b+c+(-c)≤a+c+(-c)=a+0=a.∎

Lemma 2.

If |R|≠1 and R has a characteristic, then it must be 0.

Proof.

Suppose not. Let n be a positive integer such that char⁡R=n. Since |R|≠1, it must be the case that n>1.

Let r∈R with r>0. By the previous lemma, 0<r≤…≤∑j=1n-1r≤∑j=1nr=0, a contradiction. ∎

Lemma 3.

If a,b∈R with a≤b and c∈R with c<0, then a⁢c≥b⁢c.

Proof.

Note that -c∈R and 0=c+(-c)<0+(-c)=-c. Since a≤b, -(a⁢c)=a⁢(-c)≤b⁢(-c)=-(b⁢c). Thus,

b⁢c=b⁢c+0=b⁢c+(a⁢c+(-(a⁢c)))=(b⁢c+a⁢c)+(-(a⁢c))≤(b⁢c+a⁢c)+(-(b⁢c))=-(b⁢c)+(b⁢c+a⁢c)=(-(b⁢c)+b⁢c)+a⁢c=0+a⁢c=a⁢c.∎

Lemma 4.

Suppose further that R is a ring with multiplicative identity 1≠0. Then 0<1.

Proof.

Suppose that 0≮1. Since R is an ordered ring, it must be the case that 1<0. By the previous lemma, 1⋅1≥0⋅1. Thus, 1≥0, a contradiction. ∎
